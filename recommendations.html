Business Problem 1 - Repeat Calls

Comcast has an excessive amount of repeat calls, meaning that customers call more than once within 24 hours.

Purpose

This script is a list of tools that will be used each financial quarter to evaluate call center employee performance. Using the tools will allow Comcast to evaluate individual customer service, individual call center performance and Comcast customer service. Performance evaluation will be used to identify both customer service strengths and gaps to ensure that Comcast remains a leader in the cable/service provider industry and provides exemplary customer service.

Tool 1: 360 degree feedback

360 degree feedback evaluates individual call center employees. It will incorporate feedback from internal/external customers, superiors, subordinates, coworkers, and sales people.

Tool 2: Employee balanced scorecard

The employee balanced scorecard will grade call center employees using the following quantitative metrics: percentage of calls that are repeat calls; average customer hold time; percentage of call resolution; average customer satisfaction rating; ability to connect customer with technician; ability to reconnect with customer after a blocked call.

Tool 3: Self evaluation

Employee self-evaluation will allow an employee to reflect on individual performance. It will include both quantitative analysis of their balanced scorecard and a qualitative analysis of their perceived performance. Self Evaluation will require quarterly reflection, goal setting, and encourage employees to communicate their need as Comcast employees.

Business Problem 2 - Long Hold Times

Comcast customers experience long hold times when calling into customer service.

Purpose

The following two tools are useful for improving employee effectiveness and happiness as well as create a call center that runs more smoothly.

Tool 1: Mandatory Meetings

Each month, every call center will host a mandatory monthly meeting that entails an employee workshop to touch base with all the representatives. We think this will motivate employees, build call-center camaraderie, and ensure employees are prepared to serve the customer.

8:00-8:15 Welcome and breakfast; announce winner of “Call Center Superstar” award
8:15-8:30 Overview Comcast monthly performance as a company
8:30-9:00 Overview individual call center performance and individual employee performance (not by name)
9:00-9:45 Break
9:45-10:30 Discuss most common customer problems and review/introduce how to address the problems
10:30-11:30 Open discussion by employees of common problems, questions, recommendations; employee input to call center process and Comcast customer service sector is welcome at this time
11:30-12:00 Discuss next month’s call center goals, announce the next “Call Center Superstar” challenge, and close

Tool 2: Call Center Connection Specialist

     Call Center Connection Specialists will be central to Comcast’s expanding customer service segment. The newly created Call Center Connection Specialist position has been created to quickly and accurately transfer customers to the appropriate help sectors (technology, service connection, sales, etc). The purpose of Specialists is reducing customer hold time, speeding up call resolution, and increasing overall customer satisfaction.

     Specialists must be highly motivated, enthusiastic individuals focused on serving the customer. As a specialist, you will be responsible for being up to date on common customer issues including service problems, technology setup, and package deals.

     Comcast is excited to welcome such individuals to the employee team. We expect all future employees to contribute to Comcast’s continuous growth through excellence in customer satisfaction.

Business Problem 3 - Consumption Model

Customers are given packages with many more channels than they actually want to watch.

Purpose

This issue was much more difficult to solve. We looked at a couple of different options as well as the following tradeoffs.

Current Consumption Model Tradeoffs


Improved Consumption Model Tradeoffs


We came up with two options for breaking the bundle.

Option A

Premade, smaller bundles specially designed for individual customer segments.

Example packages:

  • The Sports Fan ($30/month): ESPN, Speed, Rugby Channel, etc.
  • Music World ($20/month): MTV, VH1, etc.
  • Kid Central ($20/month): Cartoon Network, Nickelodeon, etc.
  • The Retired Gentleperson ($30/month): History Channel, Golf Channel, Infomercial Channel, etc.

Package design will be based on extensive market research. Each of these packages would contain both premium and less popular channels, but all included channels are related to the interests of the customer segment for which it the package is designed. Quantity based pricing will be used, e.g. 1 package for $20/month, 2 packages for $30/month.



Option B

Make your own Custom Package using a point system. Each channel will be assigned a point value based on popularity.

Example packages:

  • HBO = 15 points
  • Lifetime = 6 points
  • DIY Network = 1 point

Customers will choose a Custom Package Tier that allots them a certain number of points, e.g. Tier 1 = 40 points for $20/month, Tier 2 = 75 points for $30/month.
